U.S. Food Import steadily increased over the last 20 years

U.S. imports from other countries a wide variety of beverages and food including Seafood, Fruits, Grains, Vegetables, and many more.

Over the last two decades, the amount of money that the U.S. spent on imported food and beverage almost triplicated (+284%): from $58 Billion in 2004 to $165 Billion in 2021.

Population growth of approx. +0.8% as well as price inflation of approx. +4% - both in average over the last 20 years - contributed to the U.S. food & beverages import value increase. However, they were not the primary cause - Americans changing food interests is the primary cause.

Let's investigate which food categories are mostly imported before looking at Americas food interests.

At the top of the U.S. food import shopping list there are Fish & Shellfish primarily from Canada and Chile, Fruits primarily from Mexico, followed by Others Edible Products (i.e., Prepared Food and ingredients), Beverages (excl. liquors as considered agricultural products), Grains, Vegetables, Meats, and others minor categories.

Seafood and Fruits are at the top of U.S. Food Import shopping list

Now that we know that Fish and Fruits are at the top of U.S. Import shopping list, it is relevant to know that the United States imports approximately 70% of the Seafood and 60% of the Fruits consumed by the Americans.

Top seafood imports include shrimp, freshwater fish, salmon, and tuna, and top imported fruits are bananas, berries, avocados, and grapes.

Are Americans changing their diets?

The U.S. Web Searches in the Food & Drink category (source Google Trends) over the 2004-2021 period show a steadily increasing interest in Fish & Shellfish as well as Fruits.

"Fish market", "Fish recipe", "Fish tacos", "Fish near me", etc. are just some of the top Internet searches over last 10 years and clearly demonstrate that Americans love eating seafood, in particular the Coasts population that is known to be more diverse, wealthier, and healthier.
